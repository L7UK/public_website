The brief

A patient-facing service used weekly by 4 million people across the UK was scaling fast. Each release shipped new features, new third-party integrations, and a wider attack surface. The authority needed a continuous testing partner, not a once-a-year ITHC and a 200-page PDF.

What we did

We embedded a CREST-led testing rotation into the delivery cadence. Every quarter: full web, mobile and API test against the latest build. Every release: targeted retests on the diff. Every finding: a remediation walkthrough with the dev team, and an automated regression check added to the pipeline.

The hard bit

Patient data, mass scale, and a release cadence we couldn’t slow down. We had to adapt our process to fit the team’s, not the other way round. We sat in their standups, used their issue tracker, and shaped findings as actionable tickets, not as separate “security” work.

Outcome

Twelve engagements over the year. 230 findings, every one tracked through to verified remediation. The team’s secure-coding scores in our build review have climbed every quarter. Their security posture has gone from reactive to ahead of the work.
